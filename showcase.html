Roccos Dessert vegan and allergy friendly menu

Hospitality and customer experience

I contributed to the development of a vegan and allergy friendly dessert menu by carrying out research on ingredients, customer needs and dietary trends. This project helped the business become more inclusive while improving customer satisfaction.

At my previous workplace, Roccos Dessert, I took part in a team project focused on developing a brand new vegan and plant based dessert menu to support customers with allergies, dietary preferences and general health conscious lifestyles. My role centred on market research and product feasibility, where I analysed customer feedback, allergy data and current dessert trends within the plant based sector. I conducted taste testing sessions, compared alternative ingredients such as oat based creams, coconut milks and nut free chocolate substitutes and evaluated their cost, flavour and practicality in a fast paced dessert environment. The project aimed to solve the challenge of offering nut free, dairy free and fully vegan options that still matched Roccos reputation for indulgent desserts.

Through collaborative testing with the kitchen team, I helped refine recipes and identify the safest and most appealing ingredient combinations. The final outcome was a fully developed vegan and allergy friendly menu which included items such as a coconut cream brownie, plant based lotus cheesecake and a nut free chocolate fudge sundae. The introduction of this menu broadened Roccos customer base, increased accessibility and resulted in noticeably positive feedback from customers with dietary restrictions. This project strengthened my understanding of customer driven innovation and showed my ability to turn research into practical business solutions.

Student wellbeing and timetable app prototype

College digital project

As part of a college group project, I helped design a digital wellbeing and timetable app for students. I led the user research and wireframing process and made sure the prototype focused on real issues that learners face.

During my time at Holy Cross College, I completed a group based digital innovation project where we were asked to design a mobile app prototype to help students manage their academic workload, mental wellbeing and part time job schedules. My role in the team was project coordinator and user experience research lead, so I was responsible for organising task timelines, gathering user requirements and ensuring that the final design was intuitive and accessible.

To understand the core problems students faced, I carried out user surveys and short interviews which revealed issues such as overloaded deadlines, difficulty balancing work and college and the lack of a single place to track wellbeing habits. Using this research, I helped shape the prototype features which included a smart timetable, wellbeing check ins and a reminder system that adapted to the user stress level. I also sketched the initial wireframes and helped translate them into an interactive digital prototype using simple front end tools.

The final outcome was a working click through app prototype that we presented to lecturers and peers. It received positive feedback for its clear design, practical problem solving and real world usefulness. This project strengthened my skills in team leadership, user research, wireframing and digital project planning and gave me early experience in managing a technology based solution from concept to demonstration.

West Ivy problem solving page

Digital transformation for a restaurant

I developed an interactive problem solving page for West Ivy in Bury that focuses on customer engagement, modern communication and practical digital features for a restaurant.

For my third project, I created the problem solving page for West Ivy in Bury as part of a digital transformation brief where I acted in the role of chief technology officer. My aim was to design a modern and interactive web solution that would increase customer engagement, improve communication and clearly demonstrate my web development abilities. I chose a soft beige colour theme to match the overall style of my website and to create a warm and professional atmosphere.

The page includes several interactive features such as menu filtering, a live open status indicator that updates according to the user device time, a sliding image gallery, a searchable menu and a demonstration cart that allows users to explore dishes in a realistic way. I also integrated real social media links and a booking form that automatically generates an email, which provides a simple method for customers to contact the restaurant without needing a database. Every section, including the business context, the gallery, the menu, the map, the contact area and the booking form, is organised in a clear structure so users can navigate the page easily.

This project highlights my ability to combine user experience design, research and front end development to meet real business needs. It shows how interactive features, live information, well organised content and modern communication tools can enhance a restaurant digital presence and provide a smoother customer experience.
