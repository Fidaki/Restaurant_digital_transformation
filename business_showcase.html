Roccos Dessert Vegan and Allergy Friendly Menu

Hospitality and customer experience

At Roccos Dessert I contributed to a project that focused on designing a new vegan and plant based menu for customers with allergies and specific dietary preferences. The main aim was to create desserts that were nut free, dairy free and fully vegan without losing the indulgent feeling that Roccos is known for.

My role focused on research and feasibility. I gathered informal feedback from regular customers, reviewed common allergy requests and looked at trends in plant based desserts. I compared ingredients such as oat based cream, coconut milk, dairy free chocolate and gluten free bases and discussed with the kitchen which options were realistic for production and storage.

Working with the team, I helped refine a small but focused menu that made the choice clear for customers with dietary needs. The result was a dedicated Vegan and Allergy Friendly section that increased choice and made the menu feel more inclusive. Staff found it easier to recommend safe options and customers with allergies felt more confident when ordering.

Student Wellbeing and Timetable App Prototype

College digital project

While studying at Holy Cross College I worked in a small group to design a mobile app prototype that would help students manage their academic deadlines, part time work and wellbeing habits in one place. I took on the role of project coordinator and user experience research lead.

I started by creating short surveys and quick interviews with students to understand common issues. Many mentioned feeling overwhelmed by different systems for homework, shift rotas and personal tasks. From this, I helped define the core features of the prototype which included a smart timetable, wellbeing check ins and a reminder system that adjusted to preferred study times.

I produced initial sketches on paper and then translated these into low fidelity wireframes before working with my group to build a click through prototype. We presented the final version to tutors, explaining the problem, our research and the final design choices. The project strengthened my skills in planning, communication and presenting a digital solution.

West Ivy Problem Solving Page

Digital transformation for a restaurant

For my third project I created the Problem Solving page for West Ivy in Bury as part of a restaurant digital transformation scenario. In this project I treated myself as the chief technology officer and designed a page that would feel realistic for customers who are engaging with the restaurant online.

I used a soft beige theme so that it matches the rest of the site and gives a calm, modern feel. The page is organised into clear sections such as business context, gallery, map, menu, contact area and booking form to keep the journey simple for the user.

The design includes interactive elements like live opening status, a sliding gallery, a searchable menu with filters, a simple demo cart and real social media links. There is also a booking and feedback form that automatically generates an email which allows customers to contact the restaurant using a structured format without the need for a database.

This project shows how user experience design, layout decisions and front end coding can work together to support a digital transformation goal in a hospitality setting.
